Leucanthemum vulgare

The name "daisy" comes from the Old English term "daes eage," which means "day's eye." This name refers to the way the flower opens at dawn and closes at dusk, resembling an eye that opens and closes with the sun.
Daisies are also known for their ability to symbolize purity, innocence, and new beginnings, making them popular in various cultures and traditions
